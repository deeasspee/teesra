How Teesra works

Every story, dissected three ways.

01
⚖️

The Facts

What actually happened

AI reads 10+ sources per story and extracts only verified, cross-referenced facts. No spin. No agenda. Minimum 3 sources must agree before a story appears.

02
🔵

Left Lens

How the left frames it

See how progressive, liberal outlets like The Wire, Scroll, and The Hindu frame this story — their angle, their emphasis, their concerns.

03
🔴

Right Lens

How the right frames it

See how conservative outlets like Swarajya, OpIndia, and Times Now frame the same story — their narrative, their priorities, their reading of events.

Sample story card

Economy · 22 March 2025 · 7:30 AM

RBI cuts repo rate by 25bps to 6.25%, signals growth focus

Mint The Hindu NDTV Swarajya +7 more
11
Sources

Facts

RBI's MPC voted 4-2 to cut the benchmark rate. EMIs on home and auto loans will reduce marginally. Inflation at 4.1%, within target band. Next review in June 2025.

Left Lens

Rate cut welcomed but critics say it's too little — real wages still stagnant, rural demand weak. Experts worry banks may not pass on benefits to common borrowers.

Right Lens

Bold growth signal from RBI. Modi government's fiscal discipline creating room for monetary easing. Business confidence likely to surge, investment cycle to revive.

The Teesra difference

Not every story needs the same lens.

🔵 🔴 Political & Policy

Left + Right lenses weighted high

When narratives diverge sharply across the spectrum, understanding both sides matters more than public chatter.

🏏 Sports & Entertainment

Street pulse weighted high

Less political bias, more human reaction. What fans and people feel is the real story here.

⚖️ Economic & Geopolitical

Facts first, always

High stakes, high noise. We slow down, verify harder, and let data speak before opinion does.

⚠️ Sensitive & Communal

Verified facts only — perspectives with caution

Some stories can inflame. We show verified facts first and label perspectives clearly before presenting them.

Our mission

Built for India's first internet generation.

We grew up on Twitter, Instagram, and YouTube. We saw media go from neutral to loud. We watched the same event reported 10 different ways depending on who you followed.

Teesra exists because informed citizens need all sides of a story — not just the bubble they already live in.

01

Verified first, fast second

A story appears only when 3+ credible publishers have covered it. We will never break news. We will always get it right.

02

Your conclusions, not ours

Teesra has no editorial line. We show you how others frame a story so you can form your own view — not adopt ours.

03

Under 5 minutes every morning

The entire Teesra brief is designed to be read before your chai goes cold. Dense, honest, fast.
